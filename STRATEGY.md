## ğŸ† 8 Golden Rules from Toppers

1. **"Finish your syllabus once â€” however bad â€” before October."**
   Don't try to perfect one subject before moving to the next. Coverage first, depth later.

2. **"PYQs first, new problems second."**
   If you haven't solved all PYQs for a topic, don't move to new problem sets.

3. **"Every mock test needs a 2-hour post-mortem."**
   Taking the test is 30%; the analysis is 70% of value.

4. **"Your notes must fit on 2 pages per subject."**
   If they don't, you haven't distilled enough. Shorter = faster revision.

5. **"Sleep 7 hours. Non-negotiable."**
   Sleep consolidates memory. Sleeping less to study more is counterproductive.

6. **"Stop studying new topics in January."**
   January onward = only revision, PYQs, and mocks. No new topics.

7. **"Treat mock test scores like data, not self-worth."**
   A bad mock score means a specific gap to fix. It doesn't mean you'll fail.

8. **"Join a study group, but don't discuss strategy all day."**
   Discussions are valuable for doubts. But 80% of your time must be solo deep work.
